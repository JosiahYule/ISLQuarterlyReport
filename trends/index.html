Methodology
How Projections Work
  • Blended projection model — Projections are hidden for the first 7 days of the quarter. After that, up to three signals are blended: (1) Simple pace — (V ÷ D_elapsed) × D_total; (2) 7-day rolling rate — the daily increment over the past 7 session snapshots projected forward; (3) Linear regression — a least-squares line fitted to all session snapshots and extrapolated to quarter-end. When all three are available weights are 20/40/40. During the first 14 days the result is faded toward Q2 pace to dampen early-quarter volatility.
  • Posts multiplier — For impressions, reactions, link clicks, shares, and comments the blended projection is mixed 70/30 with a posts-adjusted estimate: (engagement per post) × (projected posts for the quarter). This corrects for changes in posting frequency between quarters.
  • Confidence range — Once at least 5 session snapshots are stored, each card shows a low–high range based on the standard deviation of historical simple-pace projections.
  • Followers — Uses net-new growth starting from the Q2 closing total. Rate vs Q2 compares daily growth rate against Q2's daily growth rate. The headline shows projected total (Q2 actual + projected net new).
  • Q3 Projection Timeline — Each data point represents one session snapshot stored locally in your browser. As pace shifts day-to-day the line drifts, revealing whether Q3 is trending ahead of or behind expectations. A reference line shows the Q2 final total.
  • Data is fetched live from Google Apps Script and auto-refreshes every 5 minutes.
